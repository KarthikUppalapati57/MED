POS Integrated Labor Artifacts

Toast/Clover capture clock-in, clock-out, and tip data; RestOps ingests, normalizes, and displays it.

Target Workflow: No In-App PIN Entry

Employees use Toast/Clover hardware or POS apps. RestOps becomes the back-office labor and tip visibility layer.

Integration First
Decision: RestOps should not ask restaurant employees to enter a PIN unless a customer has no POS labor integration. Toast/Clover remain the employee-facing clock device; RestOps ingests labor and tips for reporting, reconciliation, exception review, and payroll workflows.

Webhook/API Intake Workflow

Provider payloads must be verified, mapped to locations/employees, made idempotent, and stored as normalized records.

Backend Flow
Implementation implication: extend provider transforms so Toast/Clover labor events upsert `time_clocks`, while tip/sales events populate a normalized tip input table that Tip Pooling reads from.

RestOps App Views After Integration

The app becomes a display, reconciliation, and approval layer instead of a second employee time clock terminal.

Display Layer
Recommended UI direction: Time Clock page should become a synced labor dashboard. Tip Pooling should auto-load POS tips and completed hours, then let managers review, adjust with audit trail, approve, and export.
